🌸 SCRIPT

Life Insurance Alert Flow

Step 1

✨ Introduction

Agent: "Hi, this is (Agent Name) with Nationwide Life Support. Am I speaking with Mr.Ms. (Leads Name)?"

Customer: Yes.

Agent: "Perfect, thank you for confirming. The reason for my call is that I received an alert regarding your life insurance coverage, and I just need a moment to verify some information and make sure everything is up to date."

Step 2

🔀 The Fork in the Road

How did the customer respond to the alert?

TRACK A: No Coverage / Coverage is fine

Agent: "I understand. That's actually why the account was flagged. According to the information we received, there may not be any active life insurance coverage currently in place to help protect your family from final expenses or other unexpected costs."

"To make sure our records are accurate, let me ask you a couple of quick questions:"

a. If a funeral costing around $10,000 were needed today, would you feel comfortable that the expenses are fully covered, or would additional protection be helpful?

b. If something unexpected happened to you tomorrow, who would receive financial support or benefits on your behalf?

c. Who would be responsible for handling final arrangements, and have you had a chance to discuss those plans with them?

TRACK B: Already Covered

Agent: "That's great to hear. In fact, that's one of the reasons the account may have been flagged. Many policies were purchased years ago, and with rising funeral and end-of-life expenses, we're simply verifying whether the existing coverage is still meeting your needs."

"Let me ask a few quick questions:"

a. Do you feel your current coverage would comfortably cover a funeral costing approximately $10,000 today, while still providing support for your loved ones?

b. If something happened to you tomorrow, who would you most want your policy to protect?

c. Who would be responsible for handling your final arrangements, and have those plans been discussed with them?

Step 3

📋 Information Gathering

Agent: "Thank you. I've noted that information and updated your file. I just need to ask a few additional questions to determine whether there may be any benefits or options available that could be a good fit for you."

📍 You're still residing in (State), correct?
🎂 Great. And can you please confirm your current age?

Agent: "Thank you for confirming. Based on the information you've provided, it appears there may be some options available in your area that could be worth reviewing. The next step is to connect you with a licensed specialist who can go over the details and answer any questions you may have."

Final Step

📞 Compliance & Transfer Setup

1. This call is being recorded for quality assurance and compliance purposes. Do I have your permission to continue and transfer you to the licensed specialist?

MUST HEAR YES

2. Perfect. And in the event we get disconnected, is (phone number on file) the best number for a callback?

MUST HEAR YES

Agent: "Excellent. Thank you for your time today. I'll connect you with the licensed specialist now. Please hold for just a moment."

⚠️ DO NOT DISCONNECT UNTIL SPECIALIST ANSWERS
🚀

Live Agent Assistance

🛡️ Rebuttals & Objections
If "I already got covered":

“That's great to hear — glad you're protected. Mind if I ask who you went with, just so I can update our file?” (If it's vague or they hesitate, it may be a brush-off; you can gently re-engage: "While I've got you, would it be worth a comparison to make sure you're not overpaying?")

If "Not yet / I forgot / I got busy":

“Totally understand, that's exactly why I'm following up. Let me just confirm a few details are still accurate so I can give you an up-to-date number — this'll take about a minute.”

If "Not interested":

“Understood, I appreciate your time. I'll make a note. Have a good one.” (If they ask to be removed, confirm you'll take them off the list.)

If Declined OR "I Already Tried":

“Yes I understand and I hear that 50% of the time, many people are surprised to learn that being declined by one carrier doesn't necessarily mean you're uninsurable and getting a guaranteed issue plan or simple issue plan will get you some life insurance history and we can ask that they shop plans and more coverage semi annually. By doing so you agree that you consent to be contacted if we can offer a lower rate or better benefits for the same type plan? That you had made an inquiry and during my call back from your initial inquiry, would you mind telling the agent your satisfactory rate?”

If skeptical:

“That’s fair — this is exactly why we review everything carefully before recommending anything.”

If worried about cost:

“We’re not trying to create another bill — the goal is to find something comfortable and sustainable.”

If “I already talked to someone”:

“Absolutely, and this is simply a second review to make sure nothing was missed and that you’re getting the best value available.”
