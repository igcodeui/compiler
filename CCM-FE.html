🌸 SCRIPT

Customer Service Transfer Flow

Step 1

✨ Opening & Purpose

Agent: Hello My name is ______________ And I am calling from the National Life Insurance customer support center, how are you today?

Agent: And do you know the reason for our call back today?

Step 2

🔀 Customer Response

Did they know the reason for the call back?

If YES

Action: Let them explain to us that to help them solve problems and then transfer with info.

If NO

Agent: ok no problem it looks like someone from this number made an online and phone attempt into life insurance inquiry and customer support. This usually happens if you bought a plan and had questions or if you were declined for coverage, that sound correct / ma’am, sir?

🛑 IF THEY SAY NO TO THIS If no disconnect politely.
Step 3

🌉 Service to Transfer Transition

The key is making the transition from service to a compliant warm transfer.

Agent: "I'm glad we were able to narrow that down today. Before I let you go, I noticed that many clients in similar situations have found additional coverage options they didn't know were available."

Agent: "From what you said today I think its best I transfer the call, don’t worry I’ll stay on the line with you to put you on top of the que , this way you can get done what you need to quickly but also carefully". These are managing general agents so the can usually help regardless of the circumstance.

Agent: Oh and just so you know , "There's no obligation and the review is complimentary. Would you like me to connect you with a licensed specialist for a quick review?"

Final Step

📞 Warm Transfer Introduction to Agent

⚠️ Compliance Reminder

Make sure you establish they called in prior to you calling and if they argue about that or are not willing to say YES definitively do not transfer.

Agent (To Specialist): "I have Mr./Mrs. Smith on the line. They previously applied for life insurance and were unable to obtain coverage. They'd like a review of any current options that may be available. I'll stay on the line while I introduce you."

🚀

Live Agent Assistance

⭐ FE Positioning
“The biggest mistake most families make is waiting until health changes or rates increase.”
“Final expense coverage is designed to protect your loved ones from financial stress during an already difficult time.”
“Most people aren’t looking for millions of dollars — they just want dignity, peace of mind, and to make sure their family is okay.”
“Even a small policy can make a huge difference for a family.”
“The goal is protection that actually fits your budget long term.”
“A policy only works if it stays affordable.”
“We shop multiple companies because no one carrier is best for everyone.”
“A lot of people are surprised they qualify for more than they expected.”
“The licensed specialist’s job is to help you understand your options — not pressure you.”
🛡️ Rebuttals
If Declined OR "I Already Tried":

“Yes I understand and I hear that 50% of the time, many people are surprised to learn that being declined by one carrier doesn't necessarily mean you're uninsurable and getting a guaranteed issue plan or simple issue plan will get you some life insurance history and we can ask that they shop plans and more coverage semi annually. By doing so you agree that you consent to be contacted if we can offer a lower rate or better benefits for the same type plan? That you had made an inquiry and during my call back from your initial inquiry, would you mind telling the agent your satisfactory rate?”

If skeptical:

“That’s fair — this is exactly why we review everything carefully before recommending anything.”

If worried about cost:

“We’re not trying to create another bill — the goal is to find something comfortable and sustainable.”

If “I already talked to someone”:

“Absolutely, and this is simply a second review to make sure nothing was missed and that you’re getting the best value available.”

If “I need to think about it”:

“Of course — most people do. That’s why we at least make sure you have accurate information and real numbers first.”

If older lead:

“A lot of people requested information months ago and life just got busy. We’re simply following up to see what still makes sense for you today.”
